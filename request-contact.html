Take action if you think a person may be disqualified from renting

If you have reasonable cause to believe that a person is disqualified from renting, you can request a Notice of Letting to a Disqualified Person (‘a Notice’) from the Home Office to confirm this. You can then use this to help you end the tenancy.

To request a Notice, please call the landlord's helpline and we'll arrange a time to call you back to collect the information we need.

Landlord's helpline

  • 0300 069 9799
  • Monday to Thursday 9am to 4:45pm
  • Friday 9am to 4:30pm

For the callback you will need the following information:

  • property address including full postcode
  • the name(s) of the person(s) you'd like to request a Notice for
  • your role (if you are a landlord or an agent authorised to act on behalf of the landlord)

If you have any of the following information, it would also be useful:

  • date of birth of your tenant or occupier
  • nationality of your tenant or occupier
  • Home Office reference number for your tenant or occupier

If you're the landlord we will need to know:

  • your full name
  • your company name (if you have one)
  • your email address
  • your phone number
  • your postal address

If you're an agent acting on behalf of the landlord we will need to know:

  • your company name
  • your full name
  • your email address
  • your phone number
  • the agent's postal address
  • the landlord's name
  • the landlord's postal address

What happens next?

Following the call, we'll review your request and will contact you within 10 working days. In the unlikely event that you have not heard from us after this time, please call us again on the landlord's helpline as detailed above.
