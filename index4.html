4. If the tenant has time limited permission to stay

Undertaking an initial right to rent check on new tenants will provide you with a statutory excuse until a follow up check is due. This means that you will not be liable for a civil penalty.

You must do a follow-up check to make sure your tenant can still rent property in the UK if there’s a time limit on their permission to stay.

When to do a follow-up check

Do the follow-up check either:

  • 12 months after the initial check OR
  • when their permission to stay in the UK expires

- whichever is longer.

Example:
  • if a tenant’s visa expires in 9 months, you will need to check them in 12 months' time OR
  • if they are on a visa that is due to expire in 2 years' time, you will need to check them again in 2 years' time

You can get a fine if you don’t do a follow-up check and your tenant’s permission to stay ends.

You don’t have to do a follow-up check if there’s no time limit on your tenant’s permission to stay in the UK.

How to do a follow-up check

You will need to check a tenant's right to rent in the same way as you would at the beginning of a new tenancy. This means undertaking a check yourself or requesting a Home Office right to rent check if your tenant can't provide documents.

If your tenant fails a follow-up check

Make a report to the Home Office that your tenant has failed a follow-up check and you will get a statutory excuse.

You can continue to rent to a tenant once you have made a report – you do not need to end the tenancy unless you have had confirmation from the Home Office that the tenant does not have permission to stay. In a case such as this, the Home Office may issue a Notice of Letting to a Disqualified Person to enable you to end the tenancy.

You could be fined or prosecuted if your tenant fails a follow-up check and you don’t report it to the Home Office.
