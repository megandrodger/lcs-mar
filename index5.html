5. If your tenant has time limited permission to stay

You must do a follow-up check to make sure your tenant can still rent property in the UK if there’s a time limit on their permission to stay.

You can get a fine if you don’t do a follow-up check and your tenant’s permission to stay ends.

When do do a follow-up check

Do the follow-up check either:

  • 12 months after the initial check OR
  • when their permission to stay in the UK expires

- whichever is longer.

Example: if a tenant’s visa expires in 9 months, you will need to check them in 12 months' time. However, if they are on a visa that is due to expire in 2 years' time, you will need to check them again in 2 years' time.

You don’t have to do a follow-up check if there’s no time limit on your tenant’s permission to stay in the UK.

If your tenant fails a follow-up check

You must make a report to the Home Office if you find out that your tenant can no longer legally rent property in England after doing a follow-up check.

You could be fined or sent to prison for up to 5 years if your tenant fails a follow-up check and you don’t report it to the Home Office

As long as you’ve reported a failed follow up check to the Home Office, you will not get a civil penalty. You can continue to rent to a tenant once you have made a report – you do not need to evict unless you have had confirmation from the Home Office that the tenant does not have permission to stay.
